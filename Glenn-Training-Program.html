Glenn's Training Program

Strength & Definition | 4 Days/Week | Home Equipment

Profile: Male, 46, muscular build, endurance background (Ironman 70.3), kettlebells + barbell + pull-up bar
Baseline: 150kg deadlift PB, 6 dead hang pull-ups
Goal: Muscular strength and definition
Cardio: Running/swimming on alternate days (not programmed here)

Philosophy

At 46 with your background, the goal is progressive overload + metabolic density — building strength while keeping intensity high enough to drive definition. Your endurance base is an asset: you recover well and handle volume. The program leans into that with compound lifts, supersets, and kettlebell complexes that keep heart rate elevated without becoming pure cardio.

4 lifting days follow a Push / Pull / Legs / Full Body split. This keeps frequency high on major muscle groups while managing fatigue across the week.

Weekly Structure

Day Session Cardio Days
Monday Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
Tuesday Run or Swim
Wednesday Pull (Back, Biceps)
Thursday Run or Swim
Friday Legs + Core
Saturday Full Body Power
Sunday Rest

Adjust days to suit your schedule — the key is alternating lift/cardio with at least one full rest day.

Session 1 — Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

Warm-up: 5 min — arm circles, band pull-aparts, light KB press x 15

Exercise Sets Reps Notes
Barbell Overhead Press 4 5, 5, 4, 4 Heavy. Rest 2–3 min. Aim to add 2.5kg every 2 weeks.
Barbell Floor Press 3 8 No bench needed. Full range, pause at bottom.
KB Arnold Press 3 10 Slow and controlled. 3 sec down.
Superset A
→ KB Push Press 3 10 per side Explosive up, controlled down.
→ Diamond Push-Up 3 Max Rest 60 sec between supersets.
Superset B
→ KB Lateral Raise 3 12 Strict. No swinging.
→ KB Tricep Overhead Extension 3 12 Both hands on one KB.

Finisher (5 min AMRAP):

  • 5 push-ups
  • 10 KB swings
  • 5 pike press (feet elevated on a chair)

Session 2 — Pull (Back, Biceps)

Warm-up: 5 min — dead hang 30 sec, scapular pull-ups x 10, KB row x 10 per side

Exercise Sets Reps Notes
Weighted Pull-Up 4 4, 4, 3, 3 Add weight (belt/KB between feet). Currently at 6 BW — target 8 BW first, then load.
Barbell Bent-Over Row 4 6 Heavy. Overhand grip. Brace hard.
Superset A
→ KB Single Arm Row 3 10 per side Elbow drives back, not out.
→ Chin-Up 3 Max Palms facing you. Full dead hang each rep.
Superset B
→ KB Renegade Row 3 8 per side Plank tight. No hip rotation.
→ KB Hammer Curl 3 12 Strict. No swinging.
Dead Hang 3 45–60 sec Grip + shoulder health. Rest 90 sec.

Finisher — Pull-Up Ladder: Start at 1 rep, go up by 1 each round until failure, then come back down. (e.g. 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1 = 25 reps). Rest only as long as it takes to climb back on the bar.

Session 3 — Legs + Core

Warm-up: 5 min — goblet squat x 10, hip circle x 10 each, KB Romanian deadlift x 10

Exercise Sets Reps Notes
Barbell Deadlift 4 4, 4, 3, 3 Working weight ~80% of PB (~120kg). Focus on bracing and bar speed.
Barbell Front Squat 3 6 If no rack, clean the bar up. Builds quad strength + core.
KB Bulgarian Split Squat 3 8 per leg KB in goblet position. This will hurt.
Superset A
→ KB Swing 4 15 Hinge, not squat. Explosive hip drive.
→ KB Goblet Squat 4 10 Pause 2 sec at bottom.
Core Block
→ KB Suitcase Carry 3 30m per side Heavy KB. Forces anti-lateral flexion.
→ Hollow Body Hold 3 30–45 sec Lower back pressed to floor.
→ Ab Wheel / Barbell Rollout 3 8–10 From knees. Extend slowly.

Session 4 — Full Body Power

This session ties everything together. Heavier on compound lifts, built around explosive power and density. Feels most like an athletic workout.

Warm-up: 5 min — KB halo x 10, inchworm x 5, jump squat x 10

Exercise Sets Reps Notes
Barbell Clean & Press 4 5 Power clean from floor → push press overhead. Full body.
Pull-Up 4 Max-1 Stop one rep before failure. Stay crisp.
Complex A (no rest between exercises, 2 min between rounds × 4):
→ KB Swing 10
→ KB Clean 5 per side
→ KB Press 5 per side
→ KB Front Squat 5 per side
→ KB Row 5 per side
Barbell Giant Set (3 rounds, 90 sec rest):
→ Barbell RDL 8
→ Barbell Bent Row 8
→ Barbell Deadlift (moderate) 5
Finisher — Interval (10 min):
20 sec KB swing → 10 sec rest × 20 rounds Classic Tabata. Use a challenging weight.

Progression Model

Strength lifts (deadlift, OHP, row, pull-up):

  • Add 2.5kg every 2 weeks on barbell lifts when you hit the top rep range cleanly
  • For pull-ups: add reps first (target 10 dead hang), then add load

Accessory work:

  • Increase reps week 1–2, then increase weight in week 3
  • Deload every 6th week — drop volume by 40%, keep intensity

Pull-up progression (you're at 6 — here's the path to 12+):

  • Week 1–2: 4 sets of 5 (leave 1 in reserve)
  • Week 3–4: 4 sets of 6
  • Week 5–6: 4 sets of 7
  • Once at 10 reps: introduce weighted pull-ups (5kg to start)

Key Notes for 46-Year-Old Performance

  • Warm-up is non-negotiable. Your connective tissue needs longer to prep than at 25. Add 3–5 min on top of the listed warm-ups if you feel tight.
  • Sleep is your primary recovery tool. More than supplements, more than ice baths. 7–8 hrs.
  • Protein target: ~2g per kg of bodyweight. Prioritise post-session within 30–60 min.
  • Cardio days: Keep runs/swims at easy-moderate effort on alternate days — they complement this program. Hard interval cardio AND heavy lifting on adjacent days will stall recovery.
  • Lower back: With a 150kg deadlift PB and KB volume, watch for cumulative fatigue. If your lumbar feels heavy going into Session 3, reduce deadlift weight that day — don't push through it.
  • The KB complex in Session 4 will challenge your grip, shoulders and lungs simultaneously. Start with a lighter KB than you think you need.

Equipment Used

  • Barbell + plates
  • Kettlebell(s) — ideally 2 weights (moderate + heavy)
  • Pull-up bar

No bench required — floor press substituted throughout.
