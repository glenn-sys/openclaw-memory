# Glenn's Training Program
### Strength & Definition | 4 Days/Week | Home Equipment

**Profile:** Male, 46, muscular build, endurance background (Ironman 70.3), kettlebells + barbell + pull-up bar  
**Baseline:** 150kg deadlift PB, 6 dead hang pull-ups  
**Goal:** Muscular strength and definition  
**Cardio:** Running/swimming on alternate days (not programmed here)

---

## Philosophy

At 46 with your background, the goal is **progressive overload + metabolic density** — building strength while keeping intensity high enough to drive definition. Your endurance base is an asset: you recover well and handle volume. The program leans into that with compound lifts, supersets, and kettlebell complexes that keep heart rate elevated without becoming pure cardio.

4 lifting days follow a **Push / Pull / Legs / Full Body** split. This keeps frequency high on major muscle groups while managing fatigue across the week.

## Session 2 — Pull (Back, Biceps)

| **Superset A** | | | |
| → KB Single Arm Row | 3 | 10 per side | Elbow drives back, not out. |
| → Chin-Up | 3 | Max | Palms facing you. Full dead hang each rep. |
| **Superset B** | | | |
| → KB Renegade Row | 3 | 8 per side | Plank tight. No hip rotation. |

**Finisher — Pull-Up Ladder:**
Start at 1 rep, go up by 1 each round until failure, then come back down.
(e.g. 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1 = 25 reps). Rest only as long as it takes to climb back on the bar.

## Progression Model

**Strength lifts (deadlift, OHP, row, pull-up):**
- Add 2.5kg every 2 weeks on barbell lifts when you hit the top rep range cleanly
- For pull-ups: add reps first (target 10 dead hang), then add load

**Accessory work:**
- Increase reps week 1–2, then increase weight in week 3
- Deload every 6th week — drop volume by 40%, keep intensity

**Pull-up progression (you're at 6 — here's the path to 12+):**
- Week 1–2: 4 sets of 5 (leave 1 in reserve)
- Week 3–4: 4 sets of 6
- Week 5–6: 4 sets of 7
- Once at 10 reps: introduce weighted pull-ups (5kg to start)

## Key Notes for 46-Year-Old Performance

- **Warm-up is non-negotiable.** Your connective tissue needs longer to prep than at 25. Add 3–5 min on top of the listed warm-ups if you feel tight.
- **Sleep is your primary recovery tool.** More than supplements, more than ice baths. 7–8 hrs.
- **Protein target:** ~2g per kg of bodyweight. Prioritise post-session within 30–60 min.
- **Cardio days:** Keep runs/swims at easy-moderate effort on alternate days — they complement this program. Hard interval cardio AND heavy lifting on adjacent days will stall recovery.
- **Lower back:** With a 150kg deadlift PB and KB volume, watch for cumulative fatigue. If your lumbar feels heavy going into Session 3, reduce deadlift weight that day — don't push through it.
- **The KB complex in Session 4** will challenge your grip, shoulders and lungs simultaneously. Start with a lighter KB than you think you need.
