# Character Timelines — The Weight of Men

Last updated: 2026-03-25

## NOEL THOMPSON — c.1919–c.1999

- *c.1919:* Born, Freeman's Bay, Auckland
- *c.1930s:* Grows up in poverty; father dies broken and poor — origin of Noel's hunger
- *c.1940:* Brother Andrew goes to the Pacific. Noel goes to the tanneries. One comes back counting different things
- *c.1943–46:* Post-war Freeman's Bay. Builds sack business from nothing with Gordon
- *1947:* Meets Barbara Winslow at the Harbour Arms. Starts Thompson Sack Company
- *1947–51:* Barbara moves in. Lou (her son from a sailor) tolerated and ignored. Barbara falls pregnant with Gary
- *1951:* Eddie Moran deal — illegal imports through the port. Empire begins
- *c.1952:* Gary born
- *c.1954–58:* Bruce dies (tetanus, age 3). Raewyn dies (lupus, age 7). Grief becomes silence becomes law
- *c.1961:* Performance night — Gary (12), Noela (10), Neil (7) dragged before the drinking mates. Toys on the table the next morning
- *c.1965–70:* Importing operation at its height. Lou at university doing law
- *c.1969:* Tommy Walsh beating in Morrinsville pub — Lou watching. Lou begins pulling away
- *c.1972:* Gary marries Heather. Noel doesn't attend
- *c.1975:* The confrontation — adult children (led by Lou) force Noel out of the family home. He "chooses" to leave rather than lose. Barbara free at 57, but exiled by community gossip
- *c.1976–80:* Noel "rescued" by a much younger, beautiful woman who gets him to stop drinking and retire. Lives final years providing for her, completely disconnected from his children
- *c.1980:* One visit to Gary's first home in Morrinsville — meets his grandchildren for the first time. Brings expensive gifts. Makes disrespectful comments about Gary's home and Heather. Sober but still cold, still horrible, still unloving — his young beautiful wife on his arm
- *c.1999:* Dying in Auckland Hospital cardiac ward. Glenn (20) and Gary stand on opposite sides of the bed. Dies three days later, alone, after everyone's gone home — of course he does

## GARY THOMPSON — c.1952–present (age ~74)

- *c.1952:* Born, Freeman's Bay, to Noel and Barbara
- *c.1954–58:* Bruce and Raewyn die. Learns grief is silence. Learns to make himself invisible
- *c.1961 (age ~9):* Performance night. Cricket bat the next morning. Says thank you
- *c.1964 (age ~12):* Learns to recite All Black test squads for Noel's drinking mates
- *c.1967 (age ~15):* First XV humiliation. Noel's backhand after. Heather's look — first person who sees his damage and responds with gentleness
- *c.1968 (age ~16):* Leaves school. Clerk at Morrinsville courthouse — first clean thing that's his own
- *c.1969:* First visit to Heather's family farm. Cries in the car on the way home
- *c.1972:* Marries Heather. Kylie born shortly after
- *c.1974–79:* Drinking escalates. Emotional withdrawal begins. Can't connect with Kylie — never learned how
- *c.1979:* Glenn born
- *c.1980–88:* State house years — Manyrea, Manurewa, Mangere, Northcote. Police club networking. VCR incident with Glenn
- *c.1988:* North Shore house — Hillcrest. "Made it." Success feels hollow
- *c.1990:* Promotion to District Court Registrar
- *c.1993–95:* Wellington affair with Sarah Mitchell. Hotel overlooking the harbour. Contempt for Heather fully formed. Glenn invisible to him
- *1994:* Glenn's near-death (Ch 1). Gary in the back seat, hands on Glenn's chest — the truest thing about him
- *c.1996:* Leaves Heather for young PA in Wellington — only 3 years older than his daughter Kylie
- *c.1997–2000:* Drinking worsens severely. Blackouts. Getting arrested — covered up by colleagues because of his court position. Seen by Glenn's childhood friend Jeremy at a strip club/brothel owned by Jeremy's father. Gary asks Jeremy if he can get him some coke. Jeremy feels sick for Glenn — what an embarrassment. They kick Gary out of the club
- *c.1995 (Glenn age 16):* Glenn comes home drunk from a party, sees Gary sitting alone in a dark room, also drunk. The alcohol gives Glenn the courage to ask directly: *why don't you like me? why didn't you love me?* Gary, drunk, explains he couldn't show it because he never experienced it from his own father. Then breaks down crying in front of Glenn. Glenn stands up, looks down at his father crying in the dark — and feels ashamed of him. Leaves Gary there alone. No reconciliation. Just a son walking away from a father who finally broke open, too late, too drunk, in the dark
- *c.1999:* Stands at Noel's deathbed. His hand stops short of touching his father's hand
- *c.2001:* Eventually stops drinking — not AA, thinks the people in the rooms are beneath him
- *c.2002–05:* Remarries. Has vasectomy reversed. Moves to Australia. Big promotion in the courts. Has 2 daughters with new wife. Builds the life Glenn always wanted — nice home, pets, overseas trips
- *c.2010:* Diagnosed with cancer. Bladder removed
- *c.2012:* Returns to New Zealand. Buys fancy house in Browns Bay. The perfect life, finally, for someone else's children
- *c.2013–present:* Once the bladder is removed the shame and physical fallout is overwhelming. NOTE: Gary never reconciles with Glenn. No apology, no reckoning. Falls into severe depression. Attempts suicide with morphine medication. Institutionalised under the Mental Health Act. Mental health deteriorates — melancholy depressive with psychosis. ECT. Strong doses of lithium. Glenn finally sees his father weak and broken — like someone who's had a lobotomy. The man who was never present, now absent in a different way entirely

## GLENN THOMPSON — c.1979–present (age 46)

- *1979:* Born
- *1983 (age 4):* "Thriller" video. First memory of terror and fascination coexisting
- *c.1983–89:* Council housing years. Violence in the walls. Father mostly absent. VCR incident — the slap that was also the most attention he'd received in months
- *c.1986:* Dyslexia diagnosis. Learning support. The label
- *c.1989–90:* Moves to North Shore. Meets Dave. Ben Nelson fight. First cigarette in Dave's garage
- *1991 (age 12):* Stacy Charwood and solvents. First chemical oblivion. Caught by police. Gary's reaction
- *c.1992–93:* "Special 3J." Mrs Jones-Major. Cannabis becomes identity. Meets Manu
- *1993 (age 14):* Meets Michelle at Chris Jones's — Chris's older sister, ~2 years older than Chris. Knows her through the teenage years as part of the Chris's house orbit. They don't get together until mid-20s. Michelle is Dana's mother.
- *Chris Jones:* Lives two streets from the Hillcrest house. His mother is an alcoholic, father Bruce abandoned the kids. House full of boarders — strange men coming and going. Completely dysfunctional. Becomes the central hub for Glenn and his friends as teenagers — no rules, no adults, the kind of freedom that looks like freedom until it doesn't. A domestic at Chris's house (mother attacks Michelle's boyfriend, neighbours call police) sends Glenn home the night of The Dark Room chapter (Ch 15).
- *c.2000–05:* Gets together with Michelle (Chris's sister, known since 14). Dana born.
- *1994 (age 15):* Near-death from Johnny Walker — CPR on the lounge floor (Ch 1). Loses virginity. Begins selling cannabis
- *1995 (age 16):* Leaves school. Selling tinnies for local crew. Mother calls police on him
- *1995:* Car accident kills friend Mark Langslow
- *1995:* Hit by car while drunk — two days in induced coma
- *c.1996:* First rehab (Rotorua Island, 12 weeks) — to avoid stolen cheque charges. Returns to using
- *c.1997–99:* Rave scene. LSD, MDMA. Liquid acid overdose. Wellington — Hare Krishna period
- *c.1999 (age 20):* Stands at Noel's deathbed with Gary. Three men in a room for the first time (Ch 3)
- *c.2000–05:* Dana born. Corporate digital marketing career begins. Double life: professional by day, meth and GHB
- *c.2006–10:* Escalation. Meets Hannah at private rehab — introduced to IV meth. Rehabs 2 and 3
- *c.2010–15:* Legal charges — possession and supply Class A and B. Police record. Rehab 4 (Higher Ground) — discharged after two months for struggling with vulnerability
- *c.2015–18:* Rock bottom. Living in car. Electronic monitoring bracelet. Bedsit. Dana as anchor
- *c.2019–25:* Business rebuilt. Police record cleared (7 years). Engaged. Dana at university
- *c.2022–present:* Visits Gary in the psychiatric ward after his breakdown. They bond — perversely — by mocking AA together, talking about how the people in the rooms think they're great because they got sober. Two men who've spent their lives refusing to be ordinary, finding common ground in contempt. No reconciliation. Just this
- *2025–26:* Writing this. Dana is 18, leaving for university. That's why
