# Deep Psychological Profile: Intake Questionnaire
**For Personalised Mental Optimisation System**
*Structured to reveal unconscious patterns, not surface-level self-report*

---

## Instructions

Answer these questions as honestly as you can. There are no right answers. Some questions will feel uncomfortable — that discomfort is often where the most important material lives. You don't need to answer all of them in one sitting.

Don't answer what you *think* is true about yourself. Answer what you *notice* is true — especially in moments of pressure, conflict, and vulnerability.

---

## Part 1: Origins — Childhood & Formative Experience

*These questions map the developmental foundations. The past doesn't predict the future — but it does explain the present.*

**1.** When you were a child, what did you have to do (or not do) to earn your parents' approval? What did you have to suppress or perform?

**2.** Which parent were you more afraid of disappointing? What did disappointing them look like, and what were the consequences?

**3.** Describe your family's relationship with emotion. Were feelings talked about openly? Dismissed? Punished? What emotions were acceptable and which were not?

**4.** What was the dominant emotional atmosphere of your childhood home? (e.g., tense, loving, unpredictable, cold, warm, chaotic, controlling)

**5.** Was there anyone in your early life who truly saw you — who seemed to genuinely understand and accept who you were? If yes, who? If no, how did that absence shape you?

**6.** What did you learn as a child about whether the world was safe or dangerous? Where did that lesson come from?

**7.** Think of a moment in childhood when you felt deeply ashamed. What happened? What conclusion did you draw about yourself?

**8.** How did your family handle conflict? Who fought? Who went silent? Who became the peacemaker? What role did you play?

**9.** Was there a moment in your childhood when you felt fundamentally alone — not physically but emotionally? What were the circumstances?

**10.** What was your relationship like with each sibling (if any)? What role did you occupy in the family constellation? How do you think that role has persisted into your adult life?

**11.** Rate your ACE (Adverse Childhood Experience) categories honestly to yourself: physical abuse, emotional abuse, sexual abuse, physical neglect, emotional neglect, domestic violence witnessed, substance abuse in household, mental illness in household, parental separation/incarceration. Don't quantify — just notice which categories resonate and with what intensity.

**12.** Who had the most lasting psychological impact on you — positive or negative — before the age of 12? What was the nature of that impact?

---

## Part 2: Attachment Patterns

*These questions reveal how you relate to closeness, dependency, and emotional availability.*

**13.** In intimate relationships (romantic or close friendship), do you tend to move toward people when stressed and seek connection, or do you pull away and want space? Or do you do both at different moments in chaotic ways?

**14.** How do you feel when a close person isn't available when you need them? Do you panic, feel abandoned? Feel vaguely relieved you don't have to need them? Feel nothing and wait?

**15.** What's your relationship with needing other people? Is neediness something you're comfortable expressing? Something you're ashamed of? Something you're contemptuous of in others?

**16.** When someone you care about is upset with you, what's your first instinct — to fix it immediately, to wait it out, to fight back, to shut down, or something else?

**17.** Describe a recurring dynamic you notice in your close relationships. Is there a pattern that keeps showing up despite your best intentions?

**18.** Do you tend to worry that people will leave you, or do you tend to feel smothered and want more space? Or neither?

**19.** Think of the most secure person you know — someone who seems genuinely at ease in relationships without either clinging or withdrawing. How are they different from you? What do they have that you don't?

**20.** How do you respond when someone offers you genuine care or support — do you receive it easily, deflect it, feel suspicious of it, or feel overwhelmed by it?

---

## Part 3: Defence Mechanisms & Emotional Patterns

*These questions reveal how you manage uncomfortable internal states.*

**21.** When you feel threatened or criticised, what is your automatic first response? (Anger? Withdrawal? Explaining yourself? Apologising? Humour? Going intellectual?)

**22.** Are there emotions you never feel, or feel very rarely, even in circumstances where others seem to feel them? (e.g., you never cry, never get angry, never feel afraid)

**23.** When you have an emotion you don't want to feel, what do you typically do? (Distract yourself? Go busy? Think your way out of it? Drink? Exercise? Help someone else? Sleep?)

**24.** Do you tend to feel things strongly in the moment but forget about them quickly — or do you feel things slowly, long after the event?

**25.** When something bad happens, is your automatic tendency toward self-blame ("I caused this") or toward blaming others/circumstances ("that was unfair/their fault")?

**26.** How do you handle uncertainty and not-knowing? Does ambiguity make you anxious, paralysed, or do you tolerate it reasonably well?

**27.** Is there anyone in your life you find yourself idealising — seeing as special, exceptional, above criticism? When that person disappoints you, what happens inside you?

**28.** What do you most strongly dislike in other people? (Be specific.) Now: is there any version of that quality that lives in you too?

---

## Part 4: Core Schemas & Beliefs

*These questions access the deep templates that run in the background.*

**29.** Complete this sentence honestly: "Deep down, I believe I am..." (not who you present yourself as — but what you secretly fear you are)

**30.** Complete this sentence: "Deep down, I believe other people are..." (not how they should be — what you actually expect from them)

**31.** Complete this sentence: "Deep down, I believe the world/life is..."

**32.** When you succeed or are praised, do you genuinely feel deserving of it? Or does something feel fraudulent or temporary about it? (Impostor syndrome check.)

**33.** What do you most fear people would think about you if they truly saw behind the performance? What's the thing you work hardest to hide?

**34.** In what domains do you consistently hold yourself to much higher standards than you hold others? What drives that standard?

**35.** Is there a sense that you need to earn your place — in relationships, jobs, groups? That unconditional acceptance is not something you get automatically?

**36.** What's your relationship with being ordinary? With not being exceptional, special, or outstanding in a given domain?

**37.** When you imagine being truly loved and cared for — is there a part of you that doubts it's real or sustainable? Or feels you'd have to maintain it through performance?

---

## Part 5: The Inner Critic & Self-Relationship

*The inner critic is one of the most important internal structures to map.*

**38.** Describe your inner critic. How loud is it? What does it say? Does it use your voice or someone else's? When is it loudest?

**39.** If your inner critic had a job description — what problem is it trying to solve? What is it protecting you from?

**40.** When you make a mistake or fail, what is the internal process? (Attack yourself → try harder? Attack yourself → give up? Process it calmly? Deny it happened?)

**41.** How do you speak to yourself in your internal monologue? If a close friend spoke to you the way you speak to yourself, would the friendship last?

**42.** Is there a part of you that you dislike or are ashamed of? A part you keep hidden? What would it mean if other people saw that part?

---

## Part 6: Trauma & Nervous System

*These questions map how your threat system has been calibrated.*

**43.** What kinds of situations reliably activate strong stress responses in you — even when the rational part of you knows the situation isn't a serious threat? (e.g., certain tones of voice, being criticised, silence from someone you care about, unexpected change)

**44.** When you're in a highly stressful or threatening situation, what does your body do? (Heart races? Go numb? Freeze? Act? Dissociate?)

**45.** After an intensely stressful event, how long does it take you to return to baseline? Minutes? Hours? Days? Does the stress "live in your body" for a while?

**46.** Do you have physical tension patterns that feel chronic — jaw, shoulders, gut, chest? When did those patterns begin, as best you can tell?

**47.** Are there certain relationship dynamics or circumstances that consistently destabilise you — where you find yourself reacting more intensely than the situation objectively calls for? What are they?

**48.** Have you experienced periods of feeling emotionally numb, flat, or disconnected from yourself or your life — not depressed exactly, but not present? How often and in what contexts?

---

## Part 7: Motivational Drivers

*These questions reveal what actually drives behaviour beneath stated goals.*

**49.** Imagine you achieved everything on your current goal list. What would you feel? What would you still be missing? What would be the next thing you wanted?

**50.** When you're at your most motivated and energised, what's happening? What kind of work, context, or challenge creates that state?

**51.** Which of these is your dominant driver: achievement (mastery, excellence), affiliation (belonging, connection), or power/influence (impact, leadership)? Be honest rather than aspirational.

**52.** To what extent does your sense of self-worth depend on external validation — other people's approval, recognition, status markers? When did you last feel good about yourself independent of anyone else's opinion?

**53.** What are you most afraid of losing? (This is often more revealing than what you're striving toward.)

**54.** Is there something you consistently achieve at a high level but feel no genuine pleasure in? What does that pattern tell you?

**55.** What would you pursue if you knew no one would ever know about it, evaluate it, or reward you for it?

---

## Part 8: Peak Performance & Growth

*These questions map how you function at your best — and what gets in the way.*

**56.** Describe the last time you were fully absorbed in something — lost track of time, felt effortlessly competent, needed no external motivation. What were you doing? What conditions made it possible?

**57.** Under pressure, do you tend to rise (sharper, faster, more focused) or fall (flooded, paralysed, regress)? Does this differ by domain?

**58.** What's your relationship with feedback and criticism about your work? Do you genuinely use it to improve, defensively dismiss it, or collapse under it?

**59.** What's the hardest thing you've deliberately worked to get good at? What was your experience of that learning process?

**60.** Is there a domain where you repeatedly underperform relative to your potential? What do you think is in the way? (Be honest about what you already suspect.)

**61.** In what circumstances do you procrastinate? What's the function of the procrastination — is it protecting you from failure? Avoiding boredom? Something else?

**62.** Do you primarily identify with a "fixed" or "growth" orientation in your most important domains? When you hit a wall, do you typically interpret it as evidence of your limits or as information about what to work on?

---

## Part 9: Relationships & Interpersonal Patterns

*Relationships reveal the full psychological structure more clearly than anything else.*

**63.** In significant relationships (romantic, friendship, professional), what role do you most reliably occupy? (The capable one? The helper? The difficult one? The one who holds things together? The outsider?)

**64.** What's the most common way your relationships break down or create pain? What pattern keeps showing up?

**65.** Is there a type of person you are repeatedly drawn to — romantically, professionally, or in friendship — who ends up frustrating or hurting you in a recognisable way?

**66.** How do you handle someone being angry at you directly? What happens inside you? What do you do?

**67.** How do you handle conflict in close relationships — do you approach it or avoid it? Do you escalate or withdraw?

**68.** Is there a way you know you affect others negatively — something people have given you feedback about more than once? Do you recognise the truth in it?

**69.** Describe your most important current relationship (romantic, or closest friend if not in a relationship). What does it give you? What does it not give you that you want? What do you give it? What do you withhold?

---

## Part 10: Identity, Values & Meaning

*These questions map what you actually live by, not what you say you live by.*

**70.** If you stripped away all the roles, achievements, titles, and social identities — who are you? What's left?

**71.** What do you most deeply value? And then: does your current life actually reflect those values in how you spend your time and attention?

**72.** Is there a version of yourself you're trying to become that feels genuinely exciting and alive — or are you mostly running from versions of yourself you don't want to be?

**73.** What would you change about yourself if you could — and what do you suspect is the secondary gain of NOT changing it?

**74.** What's your relationship with your own mortality? Do you think about death? Does it motivate you, terrify you, or do you keep it at arm's length?

**75.** What legacy or impact do you want to leave? What do you want people to say about who you were?

**76.** Is there a story you tell about your life that explains who you are and why — a narrative of yourself? What is that story? Who is the protagonist? What's the arc?

**77.** If there's one belief about yourself that, if it were proven false, would change everything — what would it be?

---

## Part 11: Behaviour Change Targets

*These questions make explicit the patterns the person wants to work on.*

**78.** What behaviour pattern have you tried to change repeatedly and failed? Describe the cycle — what triggers it, what you do, what happens, how you feel afterward.

**79.** What would be different about your life if you successfully changed that pattern? What does it cost you currently?

**80.** What have you already tried that hasn't worked? What's your theory about why?

---

## Scoring / Interpretation Guide

*For the practitioner or the person using this for self-directed work:*

**Cross-reference questions by domain:**

| Domain | Questions |
|--------|-----------|
| Attachment pattern | 13-20 |
| Schema/core belief | 29-37 |
| Defence mechanisms | 21-28 |
| Trauma / nervous system calibration | 43-48 |
| IFS structure | 38-42 (inner critic = manager), 21-28 (firefighters), 29-37 (exile beliefs) |
| Motivational drivers | 49-55 |
| Peak performance / flow | 56-62 |
| Interpersonal patterns | 63-69 |
| Identity / narrative | 70-77 |
| Change targets | 78-80 |

**Key pattern clusters to watch for:**

1. **Anxious-preoccupied attachment + abandonment schema + fawn/fight response:** Questions 13, 15, 16, 29, 34, 47 → will show up as intense fear of disapproval, relationship preoccupation, and people-pleasing or reactive anger

2. **Avoidant-dismissing attachment + emotional deprivation schema + freeze/flight response:** Questions 14, 20, 22, 26, 30, 48 → will show up as self-sufficiency as defence, difficulty with dependency, emotional disconnection

3. **Disorganised attachment + defectiveness/shame schema:** Questions 17, 27, 33, 37, 43, 47 → will show up as chaotic relational patterns, intense shame, contradictory approach/avoid dynamics

4. **Unrelenting standards + flight 4F + impostor syndrome:** Questions 34, 36, 51, 52, 58, 60 → will show up as chronic high performance with no satisfaction, achievement as anxiety management

5. **Subjugation/self-sacrifice schema + fawn response + approval-seeking:** Questions 1, 8, 25, 35, 52, 68 → will show up as loss of authentic self, resentment beneath compliance, inability to advocate for own needs

---

*This questionnaire should be completed over multiple sessions or sittings. The most important answers are often the ones that feel most uncomfortable to write.*
